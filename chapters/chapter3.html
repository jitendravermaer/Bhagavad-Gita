Krishna explains the path of selfless action. He teaches that performing one's duty without attachment leads to liberation. Action is superior to inaction, and even wise ones act for the welfare of the world.

In Chapter 3, Krishna clarifies the confusion between renunciation and action. He urges Arjuna to act according to his duty, without selfish motives. Selfless action purifies the mind and leads to spiritual growth.
